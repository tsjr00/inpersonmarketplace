## WORKFLOW 1: Create an Account & Sign Up as a Vendor

**Goal:** Register a new vendor account on the platform.

1. Open the app in your mobile browser
2. Tap the **hamburger menu** (☰) in the top-right corner of the header
3. Tap **"Sign Up"** (filled red button at the bottom of the menu)
4. Enter your email address and create a password
5. Tap **"Sign Up"** to create your account
6. Check your email for a verification link and tap it to confirm
7. Return to the app — you're now logged in
8. Tap the **hamburger menu** (☰) again
9. Tap **"Become a Vendor"** (red text on light red background)
10. You'll see the **"Food Trucks — Vendor Signup"** page with your email shown
11. Fill in **Business Information**:
    - **Business Name** — your food truck's name
    - **Email** — business contact email
    - **Phone** — format: 555-555-5555
    - **Business Description** — what you serve, your style
    - **Product Categories** — check all that apply (grid of checkboxes)
12. Scroll down to **"Vendor Acknowledgments"** — 6 required checkboxes:
    - ☐ Freshly Prepared Food
    - ☐ Independent Business
    - ☐ Product Responsibility
    - ☐ Platform Role & Liability
    - ☐ Honesty, Legality & Transparency
    - ☐ Vendor Service Agreement (tap the red link to read first)
13. Check all 6 boxes — each one turns light red when checked
14. Tap **"Submit Application"** (full-width red outlined button at the bottom)
15. Success screen appears: **"Application Submitted!"** with a 4-step "What Happens Next" card
16. Tap **"Go to Vendor Dashboard"** (red filled button) to continue

## WORKFLOW 2: Complete the Onboarding Checklist

**Goal:** Upload required documents so admin can approve your vendor profile.

**Prerequisite:** You've submitted your vendor application (Workflow 1).

1. From the **Vendor Dashboard**, look for the **"Vendor Onboarding"** card with a red border
2. You'll see a progress bar (e.g., "0% complete") and 4 gates

**Gate 1: Business Verification**
3. Tap the **"Business Verification"** section header to expand it
4. Tap **"+ Upload Document"** (dashed border button)
5. Select your business formation document (LLC articles, DBA, business license) — PDF, JPG, or PNG
6. Wait for the upload to complete — you'll see a green confirmation

**Gate 2: Required Permits**
7. Tap **"Required Permits"** to expand
8. You'll see permit categories. For each one marked **"Required"** (red badge):
   - Tap the upload button next to that permit type
   - Select the document file
   - Wait for upload confirmation
9. Required permits typically include:
   - **Food Service License** — from your local health department
   - **Vehicle/Commissary Approval** — health department certification for your truck

**Gate 3: Market Ready**
10. Tap **"Market Ready"** to expand
11. Tap **"+ Upload COI"** to upload your Certificate of Insurance
12. Select your COI document and wait for upload

**Gate 4: Payment Setup**
13. Tap **"Payment Setup"** to expand
14. Tap **"Set Up Stripe Connect"** (red filled button) — this takes you to Stripe (see Workflow 3)

**Prohibited Items Policy** (if banner appears at top):
15. Tap **"Review & Acknowledge"** (amber button in the yellow banner)
16. Read the prohibited items list in the modal
17. Tap **"I Acknowledge This Policy"** (red button at the bottom of the modal)

Once all 4 gates show green checkmarks, admin will review and approve your profile.

## WORKFLOW 3: Connect Stripe (Bank Account Setup)

**Goal:** Connect your bank account so you can receive customer payments.

1. From the Vendor Dashboard, tap the **"Payment Setup"** gate in the onboarding checklist
   - OR navigate to the **Payments & Earnings** card and tap **"Set Up"** (orange button)
2. You'll see the **"Payment Settings"** page with a list of what you'll need:
   - Business or personal information
   - Bank account details (routing & account number)
   - Social Security # or EIN
3. Tap **"Connect Bank Account"** (full-width primary button)
4. You'll be redirected to Stripe's secure setup page
5. Follow Stripe's steps:
   - Enter business type (individual or company)
   - Enter personal details (name, DOB, SSN last 4)
   - Enter bank account (routing number + account number)
   - Review and submit
6. After completing Stripe setup, you'll be redirected back to the app
7. You should see a green banner: **"✓ Your Stripe account is connected"**
8. Verify all three status items show checkmarks:
   - ✓ Charges Enabled
   - ✓ Payouts Enabled
   - ✓ Details Submitted

If setup is incomplete, you'll see a warning and a **"Complete Setup"** button — tap it to return to Stripe.

**Goal:** Add your food safety certifications and upload supporting documents.

1. Navigate to **Edit Vendor Profile**

3. Tap **"+ Add Certification"** (dashed border button, full width)

     - 🌱 USDA Organic

     - ✓ GAP Certified
     - 📜 Other Certification

## WORKFLOW 8: Apply for Private Events Readiness

**Goal:** Submit your event readiness questionnaire so admin can evaluate you for private events.

**This section only appears for Food Truck vendors.**

1. Navigate to **Edit Vendor Profile**
2. Scroll to the bottom — the last section is **"Private Events Readiness"**
3. Read the intro: *"Want to serve at corporate events, sports tournaments, and private gatherings?"*
4. Fill out the questionnaire:

**Vehicle & Setup:**
- **Vehicle Type** — select "Food Truck" or "Food Trailer (truck + trailer)"
- **Vehicle Length (feet)** — enter total length in feet (for trailers: combined length)
- **Requires Generator?** — tap Yes or No
  - If Yes, two more fields appear (indented):
    - **Generator Type** — "Quiet / Inverter Generator" or "Standard Generator"
    - **Generator Fuel** — "Propane (minimal smell)", "Gasoline", or "Diesel"
- **Max Runtime Without External Power** — hours (1–24)

**Food Service Characteristics:**
- **Does Your Cooking Produce Strong Odors?** — Yes or No
  - If Yes: **Describe the Odors** field appears — e.g., "frying, grilling, smoking"
- **Food Perishability** — select one:
  - "Must be eaten immediately (ice cream, frozen items)"
  - "Best within 15 minutes (fried items, hot plates)"
  - "Can sit 30+ minutes (tacos, sandwiches, packaged items)"
- **Packaging Used for Serving** — describe your packaging (boxes, wrappers, bowls, etc.)
- **Does Your Food Require Utensils?** — Yes (forks/knives/spoons) or No (handheld)
- **Should Guests Have Seating?** — Yes (BBQ plates, full meals) or No (handheld, walk-and-eat)

**Capacity & Experience:**
- **Max Headcount Per 30-Minute Wave** — how many people you can serve in a 30-minute window (events use staggered waves)
- **Do You Have Event or Catering Experience?** — Yes or No
  - If Yes: **Describe Your Experience** textarea appears

**Additional Information:**
- **Anything Else About Your Event Capabilities?** — optional textarea

5. Tap **"Submit Application"** (full-width primary button)
6. Green success message: *"Application submitted! Our team will review your event readiness profile."*
7. The section header now shows a yellow **"Application Pending"** badge
8. Admin will review your answers and work with you on any gaps before approving

**After approval:** The badge changes to green **"Event Approved"** and the button changes to **"Update Information"** — you can still edit your answers anytime.

## WORKFLOW 9: Create a New Menu Item (Listing)

**Goal:** Add a new food item to your menu.

1. From the Vendor Dashboard, tap the **"Your Listings"** card (📋 emoji)
2. On the listings page, tap **"+ Menu Item"** (outlined red button, top of page)
   - If you've reached your listing limit, you'll see a "Listing Limit Reached" screen instead
3. You're now on the **"Create a New Menu Item"** page
4. Fill in the form fields:

   - **Title** * — e.g., "BBQ Brisket Plate" (required)
   - **Description** — describe the dish: portion size, sides, allergens
   - **Product Images** — info box says "After saving, you'll be taken to the edit page where you can upload photos"

   **Allergens:**
   - ☐ **"This product may contain allergens"** — check if applicable
     - If checked: **Ingredients / Allergen Information** textarea appears — list all allergens (nuts, dairy, gluten, etc.)

   **Price & Quantity (side by side):**
   - **Price ($)** — enter your price (e.g., 12.50)
   - **Quantity Available** — leave blank for unlimited, or enter a number

   **Size / Amount** (required for publishing):
   - **Amount** (number input) + **Unit** (dropdown: servings, feeds, plates, etc.)
   - Preview appears: *"Buyers will see: 1 plate"*

   **Category** (optional dropdown):
   - Choose from food truck categories (BBQ, Tacos, Seafood, etc.)

   **Available At** * — tap checkboxes to select which markets/locations this item is available at

   **Status:**
   - If approved vendor: dropdown with "Draft" / "Published" / "Paused"
   - If pending: locked to "Draft (pending vendor approval)"

5. Tap **"Create Listing"** (primary color button, left side)
   - Or tap **"Cancel"** (gray button, right side)
6. You'll be redirected to the edit page where you can upload images (see Workflow 10)

**Draft auto-save:** Your progress is saved automatically as you type (stored in your browser). If you leave and come back, you'll see a yellow banner: "Draft saved — your progress is preserved."

**Goal:** Make a draft listing visible to buyers.

**Prerequisite:** Your vendor account must be approved.

1. Go to **Your Listings** page

## WORKFLOW 13: Mark a Listing as Event-Ready

**Goal:** Flag a menu item as available for private events.

**Prerequisite:** Your vendor account must be event-approved (green "Event Approved" badge on your profile).

1. Go to **Your Listings** and tap **"Edit"** on a listing
2. Scroll down past the allergen section
3. You'll see a checkbox: ☐ **"Available for Events"**
   - Help text: *"Mark this item as available for private events. Event managers will see an 'Event Ready' badge when browsing your menu."*
4. Check the box — the background turns light blue
5. Tap **"Save Changes"**
6. The listing now shows an **"Event Ready"** badge on your public profile page

**Tip:** Don't mark everything — a focused event menu of 4–8 items is more appealing than your entire catalog.

**Goal:** Temporarily hide a listing without deleting it.

## WORKFLOW 20: View and Manage Orders

**Goal:** See incoming orders, confirm them, mark them ready, and fulfill them.

1. From the Vendor Dashboard, tap the **"Orders"** card (🧾 emoji)
   - If orders need attention, the card has an orange border and glow with a count badge
2. On the **"Customer Orders"** page, you'll see:

**Filters (top of page):**
- **Status** — filter by: Pending, Confirmed, Ready, Fulfilled, Cancelled
- **Market** — filter by specific market
- **Pickup Date** — filter by date
- **Date Range** — defaults to "Last 30 days"
- Tap **"Clear Filters"** to reset all

**Status Stats Row** — colored pills showing counts for each status

**Order Cards:**
3. Each order shows: Order #, Customer Name, Total, Date, and items grouped by status

5. **To mark ready:** Tap **"Ready"** (no dialog, instant)

7. **To reject:** Tap **"Reject"** (red) → dialog: *"Are you sure? Customer will be refunded."* → Tap **"Reject & Refund"**

**External payments:** If a customer paid via Venmo/CashApp/PayPal, you'll see a **"Confirm External Payment"** button — tap it after you've verified the payment in your payment app.

## WORKFLOW 21: Use Pickup Mode on Market Day

**Goal:** Quickly look up and fulfill orders during a market using the mobile-friendly pickup view.

1. From the Vendor Dashboard, tap the **"Pickup Mode"** card (top-left, red/primary background)
2. Select your **market** from the dropdown at the top
3. You'll see three tabs:
   - **Pending Confirmation** — orders waiting for you to confirm
   - **Ready for Pickup** — confirmed orders ready for customer pickup
   - **Completed** — fulfilled orders
4. Use the **search bar** to find a customer by name or order number
5. For each order:
   - Tap **"Confirm"** to accept the order
   - Tap **"Mark Ready"** when food is prepared
   - Tap **"Mark Picked Up"** when the customer collects their order
   - Tap **"Reject"** if you can't fulfill (customer gets refunded)

**Auto-refresh:** The page automatically polls for new orders every 30 seconds during market hours.

**Market Box pickups** also appear here if applicable, showing week number, scheduled date, and subscriber name.

## WORKFLOW 22: Set Up Payment Methods (Venmo/CashApp/PayPal)

   - ☐ **"Accept cash at pickup"** — check to allow cash payments

5. A fee notice shows: *"External payments: 6.5% + $0.15 buyer fee, 3.5% seller fee"*
6. Tap **"Save"** (blue button, right side)
   - Or tap **"Cancel"** (gray button, left side)
7. Your payment methods now show checkmarks: ✓ Venmo (@yourname), ✓ Cash App ($yourtag), etc.

## WORKFLOW 25: Respond to an Event/Catering Invitation

**Goal:** Accept or decline a private event invitation from an event organizer.

**Prerequisite:** Your vendor account must be event-approved.

1. You'll receive a notification (email + in-app): *"You've been invited to [Event Name]"*
2. Tap the notification or navigate to the event page from your dashboard
3. On the event page, you'll see:
   - **Event details:** company name, date, time, location, headcount
   - **Cuisine preferences** and dietary notes from the organizer
   - **Expected headcount per truck** — calculated from total headcount ÷ invited trucks
4. Review the details carefully
5. **To accept:** Tap **"Accept"** (primary button)
   - Optional: add notes in the text area (max 500 chars) — e.g., "We have a 3-phase setup, ready to go"
6. **To decline:** Tap **"Decline"** (outlined button)
7. Success message confirms your response
8. If you accepted, you'll need to add your event menu items to the event market (see Workflow 13 for marking items as event-ready)

## WORKFLOW 26: Review Quality Check Findings

**Goal:** Review and respond to issues found by the nightly quality scan.

1. If the scan found issues, you'll see a red **"Quality Check"** banner on your Vendor Dashboard: *"Quality Check: X item(s) need your attention"*
2. Tap **"View Details"** (red button in the banner)
3. On the **"Quality Checks"** page, findings are sorted by severity:
   - **Action Required** (red) — needs immediate attention
   - **Heads Up** (orange) — warnings to be aware of
   - **Suggestion** (blue) — improvement ideas
4. Each finding shows:
   - Title and description
   - Tap **"Details"** to expand and see more information
   - Tap **"Dismiss"** to hide the finding (it won't reappear for 7 days)
5. Fix the underlying issue (e.g., update a listing, adjust inventory)
6. When all issues are resolved, you'll see: **"✓ All clear! No issues found."**
