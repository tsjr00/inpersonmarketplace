## When You Don't Need a Platform (Save Your Money)

### Very Small Scale (Under 10 Orders/Week)

**Use instead:**
- Text message list for regular customers
- Simple notebook for tracking orders
- Cash/Venmo for payment
- **Total cost:** $0

**Why this works:**
- Low volume = manageable manually
- Personal touch matters more than efficiency
- Platform fees (6-8%) seem expensive on small revenue
- Building customer base before investing in tools

**When to upgrade:** Once you hit 15-20 orders per week consistently, manual management becomes burden.

## Detailed Platform Reviews

### farmersmarketing.app

**Pricing:** 6.5% + $0.15 per transaction (buyer pays), $10 minimum order

**Ideal For:**
- Farmers market vendors (primary focus)
- Multi-market vendors managing 2-5 markets
- Cottage food producers doing private pickup
- Vendors wanting flash sales capability
- Anyone tired of manual order management

**Unique Features:**
- 30-second pickup confirmation (vendor + customer both confirm)
- Geofenced flash sales (notify customers within 25 miles)
- Multi-market inventory management
- Private pickup locations (not just markets)
- Market manager partnership tools

**Pros:**
- ✓ Built specifically for farmers markets (not generic e-commerce)
- ✓ Multi-market native (not bolted on later)
- ✓ No monthly fees (only pay when you sell)
- ✓ Pickup confirmation prevents "I never got my order" disputes
- ✓ Flash sales increase market-day revenue 40-60%
- ✓ Pre-payment reduces no-shows from 30% to <5%
- ✓ Simple setup (30 minutes average)
- ✓ No customer app download required (browser-based)

**Cons:**
- ✗ Higher transaction fee than some competitors (6.5% vs 4%)
- ✗ Newer platform (less established than Barn2Door or Local Line)
- ✗ Not ideal for nationwide shipping or wholesale
- ✗ Limited customization (trade-off for simplicity)

**Honest Assessment:**
More expensive per transaction than Red Hen (4%) but significantly more features. If you need multi-market support, pickup confirmation, or flash sales, the extra 2.5% is worth it. If you're doing single market, basic pre-orders only, Red Hen might be cheaper option.

**Real Cost Example:**
- $5,000/month sales = $325 in fees
- Time saved vs manual: 8-10 hours/month
- Value at $20/hour: $160-200 saved
- Net cost after time savings: $125-165/month

**When NOT to use:**
- Doing nationwide farm-direct shipping (use Barn2Door or Local Line)
- Selling to restaurants/wholesale (different tools needed)
- Very small scale (<10 orders/week) - manual methods cheaper

**Try it:** farmersmarketing.app/signup

### Instagram DMs (Free but Manual)

**Pricing:** Free

**Ideal For:**
- Testing concept with 5-10 customers
- Building initial following
- Marketing and brand awareness
- Supplementing other tools

**How It Works:**
- Post products on Instagram
- Customers DM you to order
- You manually track orders in notebook/spreadsheet
- Request payment via Venmo/Zelle
- Confirm pickup via DM

**Pros:**
- ✓ Free (no platform fees)
- ✓ Customers already use Instagram
- ✓ Good for visual products (food photos)
- ✓ Builds community and brand
- ✓ No learning curve

**Cons:**
- ✗ Extremely time-consuming (3-5 hours/week managing DMs)
- ✗ No payment integration (chase customers for payment)
- ✗ 30-40% no-show rate (no pre-payment)
- ✗ Easy to lose track of orders
- ✗ No inventory management
- ✗ Scales poorly (breaks down above 20 orders/week)
- ✗ Customer service burden (answering same questions repeatedly)

**Honest Assessment:**
Perfect for starting out or as marketing channel alongside a proper platform. Terrible as primary order management tool once you exceed 10-15 orders per week. Most vendors who start with Instagram DMs eventually add a platform.

**Real Cost:**
- Platform fees: $0
- Time spent: 5-8 hours/week = 20-32 hours/month
- Value at $20/hour: $400-640/month in time cost
- Lost revenue from no-shows: ~$200-400/month

**When NOT to use:**
- Managing more than 15 orders/week
- Selling at multiple markets
- Need organized inventory tracking
- Value your time

- Vendors not doing pre-orders

- Accept cards at market

- ✓ No monthly fee
- ✓ Simple, reliable card processing
- ✓ Works offline (syncs later)
- ✓ Basic inventory tracking

**Cons:**
- ✗ Not designed for pre-orders
- ✗ No multi-market support
- ✗ No flash sales capability
- ✗ No pickup confirmation
- ✗ Limited customer communication

- ✗ Not optimized for multi-market circuit

**Real Cost Example:**

**When to use:** Running diverse farm operation with multiple sales channels

**When NOT to use:** Simple cottage food or market-only vendor

### Red Hen (Lowest Fees)

**Pricing:** 4% transaction fee, $0 monthly

**Ideal For:**
- Budget-conscious vendors
- Single-market operations
- Basic pre-order needs

**How It Works:**

- Pick up at market

**Pros:**
- ✓ Lowest platform fee (4% vs 6-8% competitors)
- ✓ No monthly fee
- ✓ Simple interface
- ✓ Basic pre-order functionality

**Cons:**
- ✗ Requires TWO separate apps for customers (ordering app + payment app)
- ✗ 71% vendor activation failure rate (vendors sign up but never activate)
- ✗ Limited multi-market support
- ✗ No flash sales
- ✗ No pickup confirmation system
- ✗ Basic analytics
- ✗ Limited customer communication tools

**Honest Assessment:**
Red Hen has the best pricing (4%), but the two-app requirement creates friction that costs you sales. Their 71% activation failure rate suggests onboarding is problematic. If you successfully activate and your customers don't mind two apps, it's a good budget option.

However, the 2.5% savings vs farmersmarketing.app ($125/month on $5,000 sales) may not offset missing features like flash sales (which increase revenue 40-60%) or better pickup confirmation (reducing no-shows).

**When to use:** Single market, very price-sensitive, basic needs only

**When NOT to use:** Multi-market vendor, want flash sales, need reliable platform

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I switch platforms mid-season?**

A: Yes, most platforms allow data export. Give customers 2 weeks notice, export your customer list, set up new platform, migrate. Expect some customer friction but most adjust quickly.

**Q: What if the platform shuts down?**

A: Always export customer data monthly (good practice regardless). farmersmarketing.app, Barn2Door, and Local Line all provide CSV exports. If platform closes, you have customer emails to notify them of new system.

**Q: Can I negotiate platform fees?**

A: Generally no at small scale. Some platforms offer volume discounts above $50,000/month. farmersmarketing.app fee structure is fixed for fairness.

**Q: Should I pass platform fees to customers?**

A: farmersmarketing.app buyer-side fees mean customers pay the platform fee, not you. This is transparent and keeps your pricing simple. For other platforms, you can build fees into product prices or charge separate service fee (communicate clearly).

**Q: What about multi-market vendors - can't I just use separate accounts per market?**

A: Technically possible but creates problems:
- Can't see consolidated performance
- Customer confusion (which link to use?)
- Inventory management nightmare
- More work, not less

Use platform with native multi-market support (farmersmarketing.app).
