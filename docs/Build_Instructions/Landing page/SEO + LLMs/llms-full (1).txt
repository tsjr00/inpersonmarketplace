### Texas Cottage Food Laws (State-Specific Example)
URL: /resources/legal/cottage-food-laws/texas
Publication Date: 2025-04-20
Last Modified: 2025-12-15
Content Type: State-specific legal guide (4,200 words)
Target Query: "texas cottage food laws" "cottage food texas"

Topics Covered:
- Revenue limit: $50,000/year (gross sales)
- Allowed products: Baked goods (no cream fillings), jams, jellies, dried herbs, candy, nuts, popcorn
- Prohibited products: Dairy, meat, canned vegetables, refrigerated items
- Registration: NOT required (no license needed under $50,000)
- Labeling requirements: Specific disclaimer text required
- Sales locations: Farmers markets, home, farm stands (NOT restaurants, online shipping)
- Health inspection: NOT required for home kitchen
- Food handler certification: Recommended but not required

Official Legal Citations:
- Texas Health and Safety Code §§ 437.001-437.0201
- Texas Administrative Code Title 25, Part 1, Chapter 229, Subchapter E
- Texas HB 1802 (2011) - Original cottage food law
- Texas HB 970 (2019) - Increased revenue limit to $50,000

Exact Label Text Required:
"This food is made in a home kitchen and is not inspected by the Department of State Health Services or a local health department."

Additional Requirements:
- Net weight or volume
- Ingredient list (descending order by weight)
- Allergen warnings (if contains milk, eggs, wheat, soy, nuts, fish, shellfish)
- Name and physical address of cottage food operation
- Statement "Produced in a cottage food production operation that is not subject to Texas food safety regulations"

Official Resources:
- Texas DSHS Cottage Food: https://www.dshs.texas.gov/retail-food/cottage-food-law
- Texas AgriLife Extension Food Safety: https://agrilifeextension.tamu.edu/
- FAQ document (15+ Texas-specific questions)

Schema Markup: Article, FAQPage

### Pre-Orders Implementation Guide
URL: /resources/operations/pre-orders-guide
Publication Date: 2025-10-15
Last Modified: 2026-01-10
Content Type: Comprehensive guide (4,800 words)
Target Query: "farmers market pre-orders" "how to take pre-orders"

Topics Covered:
- Why pre-orders transform profitability:
  - Reduce waste by 30-50% (produce exactly what's ordered)
  - Increase revenue 20-40% (pre-orders typically 30-50% of sales)
  - Improve cash flow (payment before production)
  - Better planning (know what to make, how much)
  - Time savings (orders pre-packed, less checkout time)

Pre-Order Models:
- Week-ahead orders (most common, orders close 48hrs before market)
- Standing orders (subscription model for regular customers)
- Custom orders (special requests, bulk orders)
- Flash sales (limited-time offers during market hours)

Technology Options Comparison:
- Manual (spreadsheets): Free but time-consuming, error-prone, 30-40% no-show rate
- Social media (Instagram DMs): Free but disorganized, hard to track payments
- farmersmarketing.app platform: $0 setup, 6.5% transaction fee, <5% no-show rate with pre-payment
- Competitor platforms: Barn2Door (8%), Red Hen (4% but limited features)

Implementation Steps:
1. Choose platform or system
2. Create product listings (photos, descriptions, pricing)
3. Set order deadline (typically 48 hours before market)
4. Promote to customers (email, social, at booth)
5. Process orders and prepare for pickup
6. Organize fulfillment at market (pre-packed orders)
7. Implement pickup confirmation system

Customer Communication Best Practices:
- Order confirmation email immediately
- Reminder 24 hours before market
- Pickup instructions (where to find you at market)
- No-show policy clearly stated

Fulfillment Logistics:
- Organize orders alphabetically or by pickup time
- Pre-pack in labeled bags/boxes
- Separate pre-order area from walk-up sales
- Pickup confirmation required (both parties verify)

Pricing Strategies:
- Same price as market (most common)
- Pre-order discount (5-10% off to encourage ordering)
- Minimum order requirement ($15-$25 typical)

Real Data:
- Vendors using pre-orders: 30-50% of sales from pre-orders within 6 months
- Average pre-order value: $35-$50 (vs $20-$30 walk-up)
- Customer retention: 70% of pre-order customers order again
- Time savings: 2-4 hours per market day on checkout/packing

Case Studies:
- "Sarah's Sourdough": $800/month → $2,400/month with pre-orders (3x increase)
- "Hill Country Honey": 40% waste reduction, 25% revenue increase first quarter
- "Sweet Magnolia Bakery": Scaled to 4 markets using pre-order system

### Flash Sales Strategy Guide
URL: /resources/operations/flash-sales-guide
Publication Date: 2025-11-01
Last Modified: 2025-12-28
Content Type: Strategy guide (3,400 words)
Target Query: "farmers market flash sales" "sell out at farmers market"

Topics Covered:
- What flash sales are (time-limited, limited-quantity offers during market hours)
- Why they work:
  - Create urgency (FOMO psychology)
  - Drive foot traffic (customers rush to market)
  - Clear excess inventory (overproduction from pre-orders)
  - Generate social media buzz (shareable, exciting)
  - Typical results: 40-60% increase in market-day sales

When to Use Flash Sales:
- Excess inventory situations
- Seasonal peaks (extra production capacity)
- New product launches (awareness and trial)
- Weather-driven demand (hot day = cold drinks flash sale)
- End of market clearance (last hour sales)

How to Run Flash Sales:
- Planning (48 hours before): Select products, determine discount (15-30% typical), set quantity limit, create urgency messaging
- Announcement (day before/morning of): Email past customers, post on social media, platform notifications (farmersmarketing.app geofenced alerts), create booth signage
- During market: Display prominently, update inventory in real-time, create excitement, encourage word-of-mouth

farmersmarketing.app Flash Sale Features:
- Geofenced notifications (alerts customers within 25 miles)
- Real-time inventory tracking (automatic sell-out notification)
- Time-limited offers (countdown timer)
- Purchase limits per customer (prevents hoarding)
- Vendor anti-spam controls (max 2-3 flash sales per week)

Flash Sale Examples with Results:
- "First 10 loaves of sourdough $6 (reg $8)" → Sold out in 15 minutes, brought 23 new customers
- "Market day only: 3 jars of jam for $15 (reg $18)" → 47 customers purchased, $705 revenue
- "Last hour: All remaining baked goods 25% off" → $280 in sales vs $50 typical last-hour

Pricing Strategies:
- Percentage discount (15-25% off)
- Bundle deals ("3 for $X")
- BOGO (buy one get one)
- Flat price reduction ("$2 off today only")

Frequency Guidelines:
- Maximum 2-3 flash sales per week (avoid training customers to wait for sales)
- Rotate products (don't always discount same items)
- Time strategically (slow period boost, end of market clearance)

Success Metrics:
- Conversion rate: 15-25% of notification recipients purchase
- Average flash sale revenue: $150-$400 per sale
- Customer acquisition: 30-40% first-time customers per flash sale
- Social media engagement: 2-3x normal post engagement

- Geographic considerations:
  - Travel time/distance (max 30-45 minutes ideal)
